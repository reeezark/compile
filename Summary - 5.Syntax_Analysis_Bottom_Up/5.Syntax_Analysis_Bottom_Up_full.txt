Bottom-up: Shift-Reduce [з§»е…Ҙ-еҪ’зәҰ]
вҖў Shift-reduce parsing is a form of bottom-up parsing in which a 
stack[ж Ҳ] holds grammar symbols and an input buffer holds the 
input string.
вҖў Reduction[и§„зәҰ]: At each reduction step, a specific substring 
matching the body of a production is replaced[жӣҝжҚў] by the non-
terminal at the head of that production.
вҖў Shift[з§»е…Ҙ]: During a left-to-right scan of the input string, the parser 
shifts zero or more input symbols onto the stack, until it is ready to 
reduce a string ОІ of grammar symbols on top of the stack.
6

===== PAGE 7 =====
Bottom-up: Shift-Reduce [з§»е…Ҙ-еҪ’зәҰ]
вҖў There are four actions that a shift-reduce parser can make:
пҒөShift[з§»е…Ҙ]: Shift the next input symbol onto the top of the stack.
пҒөReduce[и§„зәҰ]: The right end of the string to be reduced must be at the 
top of the stack. Locate the left end of the string within the stack and 
decide with what nonterminal to replace the string.
пҒөAccept[жҺҘеҸ—]: Announce successful completion of parsing.
пҒөError[жҠҘй”ҷ]: Discover a syntax error and call an error recovery routine.
7

===== PAGE 25 =====
Handle Always Occurs at Stack Top
вҖў Does handle appear outside the stack?
пҒөIt can, but handle will eventually be shifted in, placing it at top of stack.
вҖў Why does handle not appear in the middle of the stack?
пҒөParser eagerly reduces when handle is at top of stack.
вҖў Results in an easily generalized shift-reduce strategy:
пҒөIf there is no handle at the top of the stack, shift
пҒөIf there is a handle, reduce to the non-terminal
пҒөEasy to automate the synthesis of the parser using a table

Possible Actions[еҸҜиғҪзҡ„еҠЁдҪң]

пҒө
